OPINION- Should you buy a new macbook before Apple moves

Apple has announced that sooner than later it's moving onto AppleSilicon. Apple Silicon is the moniker for A series chips that apple has built from ground up. Historically speaking, Apple had done this once before when it ditched the PowerPC, so they have the transition experience. Considering the way in which Apple pursues its goals, its sooner than later that the transition will be complete. June this year, Craig Federighi, Senior vice president, Software engineering (https://www.apple.com/in/leadership/craig-federighi/) gave a glimpse into the the preparedness and it looked promising.

So imagine the dilemma if you buy a new mac today. You will probably end up staring at the newer macs when you visit the coffee shops and the premium price that you paid for the shiny machine today might depreciate in value faster than a toddler learns A for Apple. So unless you have a mac handy right now, no matter how slow it runs, or how quickly it gets heated up (mine does!) its better to wait and watch.

But there is one bright side to it if you want to buy a new mac today, years down the line, you might have the last of the intel macs left with you and who knows you might get an offer from a museum somewhere who are looking for intel macs.
